Welcome to HCI Concepts & Analysis

This site explores core models and frameworks from Human-Computer Interaction (Dix, Finlay, Abowd, & Beale, 2004, 3rd ed.). We focus on cognitive models, design principles, and real-world case studies that show how interaction analysis improves interface design.

Start with Key Concepts for a comprehensive overview of all HCI models and principles discussed on this site.

Key Topics

  • Norman's Action Cycle — How users perceive, interpret, and act. The gulfs of execution and evaluation.
  • GOMS — A framework for task decomposition: Goals, Operators, Methods, and Selection Rules.
  • KLM (Keystroke-Level Model) — Quantitative prediction of expert execution time.
  • Schneiderman's Eight Golden Rules — Design principles for usable, satisfying interfaces.
